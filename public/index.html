About

I am a PhD candidate in the School of Computer Science at the University of Leeds, UK. My research explores how deep learning models can be more effectively combined, adapted, and reused across different vision tasks. My current work focuses on fusion strategies for deep model fusion and multi-task learning.

Before starting my PhD, I received a Bachelor of Engineering in Computer Science and Technology from the Southern University of Science and Technology, China, in 2020. During my undergraduate studies and one year as a research assistant, I worked on neural architecture search and evolutionary algorithms, and collaborated with industry partners on related research projects.

Beyond research, I am interested in technology, investment, and understanding complex systems through multidisciplinary models. I enjoy exploring how insights from different fields can be brought together to better explain real-world problems and inform decision-making.
